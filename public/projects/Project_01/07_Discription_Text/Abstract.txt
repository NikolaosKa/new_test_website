It is rare for a city to lose a vast system of buildings and land. However, today in 2025, in the midst of climate change and industrial shifts, it is possible to imagine a near-future scenario where the urban gasoline stations distributed across our cities steadily become obsolete and abandoned. In Catalonia, this phenomenon can already be observed. Catalan photographer Xavier Aragonès documented 26 abandoned gasoline stations within the Catalonian road network in a 2011-2017 photoseries. This photoseries led our team to question what this slow
abandonment might look like within the city of Barcelona. Through multidisciplinary research methods focussing on oil leakage, soil contamination, urban policy, and material reuse, this project redesigned 3 gas stations in Barcelona while imagining future pathways for the network of 79 gas stations within the city.
